Practical technology guides for families. If you're helping a parent, grandparent, or aging loved one navigate technology, you're in the right place.

Every guide here is written for real people — not tech reviewers, not engineers. Step-by-step instructions, honest product recommendations, and zero buzzwords. These are the guides I wrote after helping my own parents (ages 78 and 82) deal with technology — from setting up phones to recovering from a $4,200 scam.

This section exists because I couldn't find anything like it when I needed it. The phone reviews online are written by 25-year-olds who've never watched an 80-year-old try to use a fingerprint scanner. The security guides assume your parent understands what a "phishing attack" is. The setup tutorials assume you enjoy configuring things. None of that is true for most families dealing with aging parents and technology.

Every recommendation here is tested with real seniors, not just read off a spec sheet. When I say "this phone is easy to use," that means I watched my dad try to make a phone call on it and noted where he got stuck. When I say "this setup prevents support calls," that's because I've done the setup wrong enough times to know what the right version looks like.

### Who these guides are for

- **Adult children** managing their parent's technology from a distance
- **Spouses or partners** who've become the default tech support for an aging loved one
- **Seniors themselves** who want to understand technology without being talked down to
- **Caregivers** who need a structured approach to tech maintenance

If you've ever gotten a phone call that starts with "I think I broke my phone" or "someone from Microsoft called me," you're in the right place.
